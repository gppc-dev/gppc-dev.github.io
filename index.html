GPPC2

The Competition

Grid-based Path Planning is a widely studied topic with many practical applications, such as robotics and computer games. It simplicity also makes it an excellent testbed for algorithmic evaluation. Many papers appear on this topic every year. The purpose of GPPC2 is to provide a common experimental setup that allows researchers and practitioners to evaluate progress in the area.

The GPPC2 is the fourth edition of the competition, with previous competitions in 2012, 2013 and 2014.

How to Participate

  • Login with a Github account.
  • Download our start kit for the classic or anyangle track.
  • Read the submission instruction of each track.
  • Submit your first entry!
  • View leaderboards of existing entries on Grid Based and Anyangle Page.

How the Competition Runs

The GPPC2 competition will run continuously until an annual report deadline. Submissions are welcome at any time before the deadline. Participants can submit multiple times before the deadline and their submission will be graded automatically.

The system will evaluate submissions over a set of benchmark problems. The evaluation includes three phases: 1) Preprocessing Phase. 2) Validation Phase. 3) Benchmark Phase. The preprocessing phase allows submissions supporting preprocessing to preprocess each benchmark map. The validation phase feeds problem instances to submissions and validates their solution. The benchmark phase times the running time of submissions.

Successful submissions will be listed on the leaderboard, together with all other solutions.

Winner Selection

There will be no winner for GPPC2. However, we will report pareto frontiers in our annual report. The authors of frontier solutions will be invited to write the report together. The annual report will be published in the International Symposium on Combinatorial Search (SoCS).

Important Dates

Submission Deadline: No Deadlines! Submit now!

Annual Report Deadline: 1st June of every year, for inclusion in the SoCS annual report.
